    What is it?

    This is an esp8266 development board. It integrated 18650 charging system, OLED display and three buttons. It is a device which performs a death attack. You select the clients you want to disconnect from their network and start the attack. As long as the attack is running, the selected devices are unable to connect to their network. Other attacks also have been implemented, such as beacon or probe request flooding.

    Why did you make it?

    I designed this development board for spacehuhn's great project: esp8266_deauther

    It has already been authorized by spacehuhn.

    Thanks to the feedback of customers. I will keep improving this board.

    How it works

    The 802.11 Wi-Fi protocol contains a so called de-authentication frame. It is used to disconnect clients safely from a wireless network.

    Because these management packets are unencrypted, you just need the mac address of the Wi-Fi router and of the client device which you want to disconnect from the network. You don’t need to be in the network or know the password, it’s enough to be in its range.

    Please check the legal regulations in your country before using it. What it does, how it works and how to protect against it is described on the GitHub page (link above).

    What makes it special?

    • V3 will include an 8dB antenna and an acrylic case.
    • Navigation button changed (Up-D6,Down-D7, Select-D5).
    • 7 GPIOs break out(AD,D0,D3,D4,D8,RX,TX) with 3V/5V output.
    • LED indicator: There are 4 LEDs show the battery status in real time.
    • Power button: Click one time will start. Fast click 2 times will shut down.
    • Protection: Short protection. Over charging protection. Over discharging protection. Temperature protection.
    • OLED display(D1-SDA,D2-SCL.)
